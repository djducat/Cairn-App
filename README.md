# Cairn

Cairn brings classic parser-driven interactive fiction into a modern Mac experience.

It grew out of a lifelong affection for Infocom games and the pull to play them again with a focused desktop workspace, library tools, automatic mapping, iCloud continuity, and reading themes that make long sessions easier to return to. Cairn keeps the familiar rhythm of typing commands and reading the response while adding the support tools that feel natural on a modern Mac.

## What Cairn Does

Cairn is a macOS interactive fiction player and library manager for Infocom-style adventures. The app is designed to honor the original feel of parser-driven storytelling while making long-form play more immersive, continuous, and easier to resume.

The current experience brings the story transcript, command entry, collection context, and map support into a focused desktop workspace, so returning to a long, intricate text adventure feels natural on a modern Mac.

## Highlights

- **Library management**: Organize, browse, and revisit games through a cleaner collection experience.
- **iCloud synchronization**: Keep supported library state and play context available across Macs signed into the same Apple account.
- **Automatic mapping**: Reduce note-taking overhead with built-in support for tracking rooms, movement, and explored spaces.
- **macOS multi-space support**: Spread the play session, map, and library across desktop spaces when focus helps.
- **Custom themes**: Tune the reading environment for long sessions and personal preference.
- **Native Mac experience**: Built for macOS with desktop windowing, menu commands, and a quiet, readable interface.

## Compatibility

Cairn is a Mac app for macOS.

Current story-file support is focused on Z-machine interactive fiction, with the initial release centered on Z3 story files. Broader Z-machine version support is planned as the interpreter continues to mature.

No commercial games are included with Cairn. You are responsible for adding story files you have the right to use.

## Privacy

Cairn is designed to keep your interactive fiction library, play state, and reading context under your control.

- App data is stored locally on your Mac.
- iCloud synchronization is used only when supported and enabled by your Apple account and device settings.
- Cairn does not sell or share your library, transcripts, notes, maps, or play history.
- Support information you voluntarily provide is used only to respond to your inquiry.

For privacy questions, contact [support@ducat.dev](mailto:support@ducat.dev).

## Development Notes

Cairn is also a deliberate exercise in learning how to build well with agentic AI. Infocom called its creators "Implementers" rather than developers, and that framing fits the project: shaping the app from a developer's point of view, with careful product judgment, architecture, implementation direction, and technical oversight.

The goal is useful, high-quality software that respects the original spirit of interactive fiction while making it more comfortable to play today.
